KedarNath

Kedarnath Dham, located in the Garhwal Himalayan ranges, has a rich history dating back to the ancient ages of Hindu religion.0 The temple is believed to have been built by the Pandavas and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest Hindu shrines of Shiva.1 There are various versions about the origin of the temple, with some claiming it was built by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century and others by Raja Bhoj of Malwa in the 2nd century.

The temple is not directly accessible by road and has to be reached by a 22 km uphill trek from Gaurikund. Pony, mule, and manchan service is available to reach the temple.1 The history of Kedarnath Dham can be traced back to the great sage, saint, and philosopher Adi Shankaracharya, who established a number of mutths, temples, and pilgrimages throughout the way from his birthplace in Kerala to Kedarnath

BadriNath

Badrinath Temple was originally a Buddhist shrine until the 8th century when Adi Shankara converted it to a Hindu temple.1 According to Skand Puran, the idol of Lord Badrinath was recovered by Adiguru Shankaracharya from Narad Kund and re-enshrined in this temple.2 The temple has historical significance because it was installed by Adi Shankara and he set up his own people there

Today, the descendants of the families that he set up traditionally, the Nambudiris, are the priests in the temple.0 The temple has a brightly colored exterior similar to that of Buddhist temples, leading to the argument that the architecture of the temple resembles a Buddhist temple

Haridwar

Haridwar is a city with a rich history and spiritual significance.0 The first modern-era written evidence of Haridwar is found in the accounts of a Chinese traveller, Huan Tsang, who visited India in 629 CE. During the reign of King Harshavardhan, Haridwar was recorded as 'Mo-yu-lo', which still exists at Mayapur, a little to the south of the modern town. Among the ruins are a fort and three temples, decorated with broken stone sculptures

2 The Chandi Devi Temple, also known as Neel Parvat Teerth, is one of the Panch Tirth (Five Pilgrimages) located within Haridwar.1 The temple was built under the former Shankaracharya Maha-Mandleshwar Swami Satyamitranand Giri Maharaj.2 The Maya Devi Temple is the site where the navel of Goddess Sati fell while Shiva was transporting her body. The current temple dates back to the 11th century and may have been built on a possibly more ancient temple

Rishikesh

Rishikesh was part of the legendary "Kedarkhand" mentioned in the Skanda Purana.[16] Legends state that Lord Rama did penance here for killing Ravana, the asura king of Lanka. Lakshmana, Rama's younger brother, crossed the Ganges using two jute ropes at the point where the present Lakshman Jhula (लक्ष्मण झूला) suspension bridge stands today. Lakshman Jhula also collapsed in 2020. And a much stronger, 2nd in the whole of India, a glass bridge, is being built across the river. [17] The 248-foot long iron-rope suspension bridge built in 1889 was washed away by flooding in 1924. In 1927, it was replaced by the current, stronger bridge built by the United Provinces Public Works Department, connecting the Tapovan, Tehri, and Jonk, Pauri Garhwal districts.

The Skanda Purana also mentions the site as "Indrakund" where Indra underwent a holy bath to remove a curse.The Ganges, one of the most sacred rivers to Hindus, flows through Rishikesh in its course from the Shivalik Hills of the Himalayas to the plains of northern India with temples built along the banks[3] Shatrughna Mandir, Bharat Mandir, and Lakshman Mandir are the ancient temples established by Adi Shankaracharya. Shatrughna Temple is near the Ram Jhula suspension bridge, while Lakshman Mandir is situated near the Lakshman Jhula suspension bridge.
